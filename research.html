Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of high-energy astrophysics, observation, and scientific computing. I am particularly interested in the methods that connect X-ray detector measurements to robust physical interpretation.

Source characterization

I am interested in measuring the position, brightness, spectrum, and time behavior of X-ray sources and in understanding how these observations constrain their physical nature.

Response and calibration

Detector response, point-spread functions, and calibration choices enter an analysis before a physical model is evaluated. Making those effects explicit is central to reliable inference.

Statistical modeling

Forward-folded models and careful uncertainty treatment provide a principled way to compare physical theories with the counts recorded by a detector.

Research practice

I value documented assumptions, explicit units, readable code, and results that can be reproduced from the original data products.
